P194. Let $n$ be an integer greater than 2. Find the greatest real number $C_{\text{min}}$ and the least real number $C_{\text{max}}$ such that for any positive real numbers $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ (with $x_n = x_0$ and $x_{n+1} = x_1$), the following inequality holds:
$$

$$

S194. $C_{\text{min}} = \frac{1}{2(n-1)}$
$C_{\text{max}} = \frac{1}{2}$

We will prove that $m_{n}=\\frac{1}{2(n-1)}, M_{n}=\\frac{1}{2}$. First, let us see that the inequality\n\n$$\n\\sum_{i=1}^{n} \\frac{x_{i}}{x_{i-1}+2(n-1) x_{i}+x_{i+1}} \\geq \\frac{1}{2(n-1)}\n$$\n\nis trivial, because $x_{i-1}+2(n-1) x_{i}+x_{i+1} \\leq 2(n-1) \\cdot \\sum_{k=1}^{n} x_{k}$ for all $i$.
