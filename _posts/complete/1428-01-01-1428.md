Q2. Suppose the government has established the following basic policy regarding whether to proceed with four national projects, A, B, C, and D: If A is carried out, B will also be carried out. If C is carried out, D will also be carried out. At least one of A or C will be carried out. Which of the following is not necessarily true?

① At least two projects will be carried out.
② If it is decided not to carry out A, then exactly two projects will be carried out.
③ If it is decided not to carry out B, then C will be carried out.
④ If it is decided not to carry out C, then B will be carried out.
⑤ If it is decided not to carry out D, then whether the other three projects will be carried out is also completely determined.

A2. ②

B2. Gathering all the true statements you can obtain: A → B, ~A → C, ~A → D, ~B → ~A, ~B → C, ~B → D, C → D, ~C → A, ~C → B, ~D → A, ~D → B, ~D → ~C
